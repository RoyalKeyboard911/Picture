Happy 41st Birthday, Mama

Celebrating love, time, and quiet shared moments
Made with ♥
Your loving child

It’s crazy how it’s already been a year. I’ll admit I had no idea what to do this year.

I’ve been scrambling way worse than any year before without a clue in the world on what to do and how to make this year special for you. That’s when I realized the answer was hiding right in plain sight. I just needed a Surabhi Masi to ring the bell. You told me how much you adored watching old Hindi movies with me.

So this year, my gift for you is spending quality time with you. Whenever you feel up to it and whenever I’m free, we can spend time together watching your favorite nostalgic movies on the couch at home. Popcorn and nachos included. We can’t watch a movie without those, can we now?

Now it’s time for the sappy part of this wish that makes me cringe every year when I come back to read what I wrote last year for inspiration. I’ve realized that even though it’s just the two of us together, we barely get any time to spend together. I know how busy you get with work, how hard it is to run the house, how hard it is to move and still come back with a smile every day.

I truly understand some nights, some days, we can’t spend together. I might be frustrated at the time, but deep down inside, I know you’re just trying your best. And whether you’re swamped and exhausted, you’ve always made time when I need you the most. Whether I’m stressed about a grade, a test, or just nervous, you’ve always been there to console me when I need you most.

I want you to know I will cherish those moments of comfort and warmth forever, and I truly do appreciate how you take time whenever I need it the most.

♥
I love you, Mama, and I hope you enjoy your birthday to the fullest. I want you to know that I’ll always be grateful for everything you do for me — even on the days when I’m too caught up to show it. Your love stays with me, even when my words fall short.
